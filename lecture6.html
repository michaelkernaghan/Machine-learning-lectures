6: Introduction to Text Embeddings in Vector Spaces

Introduction to Vectors

What is a Vector?

  • A vector in mathematics and physics is a quantity that has both magnitude (or length) and direction.
  • Unlike scalars, which have only magnitude, vectors help us represent quantities that direct towards a particular direction.

Components of Vectors

Direction

  • The direction of a vector describes the line along which it lies. It is often indicated by an arrow where the vector points from the origin to a specific point.

Length (Magnitude)

  • The length of a vector represents the size of the vector. It is calculated from the origin to the endpoint of the vector.
  • Consider a vector v = (3, 4).
  • Length: √(3² + 4²) = 5
  • This vector extends 3 units in the x-direction and 4 units in the y-direction.

Application to Word Embeddings

From Vectors to Word Embeddings

  • In natural language processing, words can be transformed into vectors in a multi-dimensional space.
  • This transformation allows us to measure the similarity between words based on the geometric distance between their vectors.

Numerical Example

  • Imagine "king" and "queen" as vectors in a 300-dimensional space.
  • The similarity in their meanings is reflected in the closeness of their vectors in this high-dimensional space.

Conclusion

Understanding vectors is fundamental to exploring more complex models, such as those used in machine learning and deep learning for natural language processing.
